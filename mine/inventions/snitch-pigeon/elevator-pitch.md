# Snitch Pigeon — Elevator Pitch

Tinder has 75 million users. A lot of them are already in relationships.

Snitch Pigeon is an AI loyalty companion — a desktop pet that lives on both partners' devices simultaneously. When it detects suspicious activity like a new dating profile or unusual app behavior, it flies to the other partner's screen and delivers the tip in character: quietly, personally, with just enough deniability to feel like a friend pulling you aside rather than an alarm going off.

It's not surveillance. It's a heads-up from a bird who just happened to notice.

The core product is the pigeon itself — animated, reactive, personality-driven. Users choose a skin: Street, Ivy, Sifu, Holler, and more. Each one delivers the same information in a completely different voice. The bad news doesn't change. The medicine does.

When there's nothing to report, he sits on his little stool and minds his business.

He's a stool pigeon. That's the whole bit.

The market is couples who want a layer of soft accountability without turning their relationship into a surveillance state. The hook is humor and personality. The retention is trust — because once he's saved you once, you'll never uninstall him.
