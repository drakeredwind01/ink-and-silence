## Author's intentional techniques — do not "fix" these

1. **Dual-terminology alternation.** Synonym pairs (game/session/protocol/play/activity) are deliberately used in both lead-orders across the document — e.g. "session... play" in one place, "play... activity" in another. This is a mnemonic device: the author was trained by their mother to vary phrasing when reading aloud/writing rather than repeat one term, reinforced by the "say it multiple ways" principle from scripture. Don't propose collapsing these to one consistent term — that would undo the technique.
2. **Register-mixing.** Plain/visceral voice for personal-danger warnings ("if it wants to break your arm") vs. clinical/tactical voice for procedural subsections (camera setup; the Counting & Alphabet Trap negotiation in Phase 3 — "threat assessment," "operational parameters"). This split tracks with content type (visceral warning vs. technical procedure) and is a real pattern worth extending, not an accident to homogenize.
