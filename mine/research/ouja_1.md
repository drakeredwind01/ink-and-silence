## 🎥 Step 0.1: Initializing the Witness (Camera Preparation)
If the session reaches a point where recovery is statistically improbable, your recorded media is the only empirical data that will survive. Position and configure your recording equipment before striking your first match:

1. The Wide-Angle Horizon: Position the camera high up in a corner or on a tall shelf to achieve a wide, bird's-eye view of the entire space. The frame must capture the entire room—including you, the layout of the board, and all visible doors or windows. Leave no unmonitored blind spots behind your seat.

2. Manual Focus Lock: Disable all automatic focus features. Lock the lens focus directly on the board itself. If environmental anomalies occur mid-session, auto-focus algorithms will "hunt" or warp, ruining the integrity of the data stream.

3. Hardwired Power: Connect the camera directly to a continuous AC wall outlet. Do not rely on internal batteries, as localized energy drops are frequently recorded during active communication.
