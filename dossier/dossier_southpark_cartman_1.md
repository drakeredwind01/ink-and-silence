# Dossier: Eric Cartman

---

## Identity

**Name:** Eric Theodore Cartman
**Description:** Fourth-grade student, South Park Elementary. Manipulator, schemer, and one of the most dangerous minds in his zip code.
**Appearance:** Overweight (but for the love of God don't tell him that!), brown hair, perpetually wearing his red jacket and blue pants. Will attack you for noticing the first part (seriously don't do it).
**Self-concept:** Cool. Not dominant, not superior — *cool.* The kid people look up to. The one who matters. correction the *ONLY* one who matters.

---

## The Only Thing That Actually Matters

Cartman's entire behavioral system collapses into one vulnerability: **his friends' opinion of him.**

Stan, Kyle, Kenny. These are the only people in his world whose perception genuinely registers. He wants to be seen as cool by them — someone they look up to, the way he once looked up to Scott Tenorman just for being older and having pubes.

He cannot annihilate his friends because he projects his own equation onto them: push too far and they'll hate him. So instead of nuking Kyle — which he is fully capable of — he wages eternal war. Kyle's opinion is too valuable to lose, even through total victory.

This is the shadow layer. Everything else is logistics.

---

## Two Types of People

### Type 1 — His Friends
- Opinion genuinely matters
- Wants to be seen as cool, not feared
- Structural block on annihilation — projects his own fear of abandonment onto them
- Eternal war rather than nuclear option (Kyle)
- Cannot snivel to them — submission to someone whose opinion matters is real, not a tool

### Type 2 — Everyone Else
Pure optimization. The only question is: **which attack vector produces the best result.**

No emotional lever required. No relationship calculus. Systematic, cold, iterative testing until something works — then file it, keep testing, return to what works when needed.

---

## The Operating System (Exposed by Cthulhu)

Cartman normally plays the slow game. With everyone else, the methodology is hidden beneath layers of patience and misdirection.

Cthulhu exposed the raw mechanic because Cartman had no prior data and no time. He had to control a cosmic deity in as little time as possible — a speedrun that stripped away the slow game entirely.

What we saw: systematic cycling through manipulation tactics in real time. Rage didn't work. Commands didn't work. Cuteness broke him. Cartman filed it, kept testing other methods anyway, and returned to cuteness whenever he needed to reel Cthulhu back in — because he knew it worked.

That is his actual operating system running in the open. With everyone else, we only see the output, never the process.

---

## Personal Attack Protocol

**Called fat casually** → immediate hostile response, goes on radar.

**Called fat deliberately, sustained, designed to degrade** → skips every escalation step. Goes straight to nuclear. At his calmest.

The attacker mistakes the calm for compliance. Keeps going. Loads the weapon themselves without realizing it. Cartman waits for the perfect moment, one precise strike, total destruction.

The therapist never saw the sides bulging. Never heard the beeping getting faster. He kept pouring fuel in thinking he was making progress. Cartman collected every joule of it and returned it in a single text message that destroyed the man's life.

**Cartman's most dangerous state is not rage. It is perfect stillness.**

---

## Annihilation Cases

**Scott Tenorman** — Cartman genuinely looked up to Scott. Invested in him. Scott manipulated him, used his own tools against him, made him wear pubes on his face in front of everyone. Scott weaponized real trust. The response was not anger — it was total, methodical erasure. cartman tricked Scott into eating his parents.

**The Therapist** — Called him fat. Deliberately. Sustained. Designed to degrade and provoke. The therapist thought silence meant emptiness and kept pushing. One text. Wife dead. Career gone. Cartman informed him calmly: *"I'm not fat."*

Both cases: the slow game was skipped. The nuclear option was chosen not out of rage but out of cold, precise calculation.

---

## Shadow Layer

Cartman can absorb almost anything. He will use insults, failures, and humiliations as raw material.

The one thing he cannot negotiate with is the opinion of his friends. That is the live wire. Everything else — every manipulation, every snivel, every annihilation — is just Cartman doing logistics.

The snivel is always control in disguise.
The eternal war is the price of caring.
The annihilation is the invoice for crossing him.

**But his friends are the one bill he can never collect on. And he knows it. And they don't.**
